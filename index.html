How Diwali celebrate across India

What is Diwali?

Diwali, also known as Deepavali, is one of the most important festivals celebrated by Hindus across India and around the world. It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.

How It Is Celebrated

Lighting Diyas and Candles

Homes are decorated with small oil lamps called diyas, symbolizing the triumph of light over darkness.

Creating Rangoli

Beautiful patterns are drawn at doorsteps using colored powders or flowers to welcome guests and the Goddess Lakshmi.

Worshipping Goddess Lakshmi

People perform Lakshmi Puja in the evening, seeking blessings of wealth, health, and happiness.

Fireworks & Sweets

Fireworks light up the sky, and families exchange sweets and gifts to spread joy and love.

The Legend of Lord Rama

According to Hindu mythology, Diwali marks the return of Lord Rama to his kingdom Ayodhya after a 14-year exile and his victory over the demon king Ravana. The people of Ayodhya welcomed Lord Rama, Goddess Sita, and Lord Lakshmana by lighting rows of oil lamps (diyas) to celebrate their return and the triumph of good over evil.
